  • Analytics can help Businesses achieve the following:
  • Understand their Users/Customers
  • Track health of a product or feature
  • Determine if they are achieving growth
  • Keep Cross-functional Teams aligned on roadmaps and goals
  • Uncover Areas of Opportunity and Improvement
  • Forecast future trends
  • Build More Effective Products
  • Reduce biased perspectives

  • Avoid HIPPO (Highest Paid Person’s Opinion)

  • All of your work will be done in context of specific business and product objectives
  • Ensures your work is consistently aligned with goals of the company
  • BI and Product Analytics Roles:
  • Identify Key Metrics, develop insights and product Recommendations that will have the biggest impact
  • Machine Learning driven roles:
  • Develop ML models in that are in alignment with company goals, scalable and adaptive to changing needs and usage patterns
  • Product Sense can inform feature engineering, segmentation, etc

  • Allows you to effectively use and communicate data analysis to stakeholders and drive important business and product - decisions

  • Deeply understanding the goals of a product, both for the business and the customer.
  • Constantly evaluating how well the product is serving those goals, and what elements contribute or detract.
  • Finding opportunities to better serve these goals.

  • Understand the best set of metrics to use and deliver key insights that help drive actionable recommendations for the product or business

  • A useful metric is a figure that is directly measurable and can be used to track performance over time
  • Quantities
  • eg. ‘Likes” per post, Daily unique visitors, pages viewed per session
  • Calculated values
  • eg. Revenue Per Sale, Weekly retention
  • Discrepancies

  • eg. Comparisons by week before, baseline averages, cohort views

  • General Tips
  • Ask Clarifying Questions
  • Explore potential markets, customer segments, business impact
  • Agree on GOAL with your interviewer
  • Identify Key Metrics
  • Turn goal into a measurable item(s)
  • Eg. If engagement is of concern what metrics can be used to measure that?
  • Daily Average number of likes per user on Facebook

  • Don’t forget that Revenue is the ultimate goal for any company

  • Scope potential Data Points and Variables
  • What variables may help move the metric?
  • How data can identify areas for simplification?
  • Develop hypothetical examples (eg. specific market that may behave differently)
  • Define Next Steps and Recommendations
  • Describe how would you track success of your efforts
  • A/B Testing

  • What Success Metrics would you use?

Engagement Metrics

Active user metrics are especially important for monetization with ad revenue.

Daily active users: \[ DAU = n_{users,day} \]

Average daily active users: \[ \begin{aligned} ADAU &= \frac{\text{total number of active users in a month}}{\text{days in the average month}}\\ &= \frac{\sum_{days}^{n_{days}} DAU_{day}}{n_{days}} \end{aligned} \]

Monthly active users: \[ \begin{aligned} MAU &= \frac{\text{total number of active users in a year}}{12} \\ &= \frac{\sum_{days}^{365} DAU_{day}}{n_{months}} \end{aligned} \]

Stickiness: Here the numerator is a measure of activity within a month, and the denominator is a measure of activity within a year. \[ \begin{aligned} S &= \frac{ADAU}{MAU} \\ &= \frac{\frac{1}{n_{days}}\sum_{days}^{n_{days}} DAU_{day}}{\frac{1}{n_{months}}\sum_{days}^{365}DAU_{day}}\\ &= \frac{n_{months}\sum_{days}^{n_{days}}DAU_{day}}{n_{days}\sum_{days}^{365}DAU_{day}} \end{aligned} \]

alternatively, stickiness could be measured as \(DAU\) over \(MAU\)

  • Active users: Average Daily Active Users = monthly users / days in average month
